🔍 What Is OCR and How Does It Work?

OCR stands for Optical Character Recognition. It is technology that identifies and extracts text characters from images, converting them into editable and searchable digital text. OCR has been around since the 1950s but has become significantly more accurate with modern machine learning approaches.

ToolsNest's OCR tool is powered by Tesseract.js — an open-source OCR engine originally developed by HP and now maintained by Google. Critically, Tesseract.js runs entirely inside your browser using JavaScript. Your image is never sent to any external server, making it completely private even for sensitive documents.

How Tesseract works: The engine analyses your image pixel by pixel, identifies patterns that correspond to text characters, groups them into words and lines, and outputs the recognised text. Higher image quality and contrast directly improves recognition accuracy.

✅ Tips for Best OCR Results

  • 1
    Use the highest resolution image available. Blurry or low-resolution images are the single biggest cause of poor OCR accuracy. If photographing a document, get as close as possible with a steady hand.
  • 2
    Ensure strong contrast between text and background. Dark text on a white or light background gives the best results. Coloured backgrounds, watermarks, and decorative patterns reduce accuracy.
  • 3
    Shoot straight-on, not at an angle. OCR engines struggle with perspective distortion. Hold your camera directly above the document, parallel to the page, for the cleanest result.
  • 4
    Good lighting is essential. Avoid shadows across the text. Natural light or even overhead room lighting works well. Flash can create glare on glossy documents — use ambient light instead.
  • 5
    Crop out unnecessary areas. Before uploading, crop your image to show only the text area you need. Reducing noise around the text helps the OCR engine focus on what matters.
  • 6
    For scanned PDFs, use a dedicated tool. If your PDF already contains embedded text, our PDF to Word converter will extract it more accurately than OCR on a screenshot of the PDF.
💡 Pro tip: Screenshots always produce better OCR results than photos of screens. If you're extracting text from a website, app, or document on your computer — take a screenshot rather than photographing your monitor.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

Is my image uploaded to a server?
No — never. This tool uses Tesseract.js, which runs the OCR engine entirely inside your browser using JavaScript. Your image never leaves your device and is never sent to any external server. This makes it completely safe for confidential documents like contracts, medical records, and financial files.
What is OCR?
OCR stands for Optical Character Recognition. It is technology that scans images pixel by pixel, identifies patterns corresponding to text characters, and outputs those characters as editable digital text. Modern OCR engines like Tesseract are extremely accurate on high-quality images.
What image formats are supported?
This tool supports JPG, PNG, WebP, GIF, and BMP image formats. For best OCR accuracy, use high-resolution JPG or PNG images with strong contrast between the text and background.
Why is my extracted text not accurate?
OCR accuracy depends heavily on image quality. The most common causes of poor accuracy are: low resolution, blurriness, poor lighting, extreme camera angles, low contrast between text and background, and decorative or coloured backgrounds. Use a high-resolution, well-lit, straight-on image for best results.
What languages does this OCR tool support?
This tool currently supports English text recognition. It works best with clear, printed text in standard fonts. Handwritten text, decorative fonts, and non-Latin scripts may produce lower accuracy.
Can I extract text from a PDF?
This tool works with image files. If your PDF is text-based (you can select text in it), use our PDF to Word converter for much more accurate extraction. If your PDF is a scanned image, take a screenshot of each page and run it through this OCR tool.
Can I extract text from a photo of a book or whiteboard?
Yes — this is one of the most common uses. For best results, photograph the page straight-on with good lighting and no shadows. A distance of 30–50cm usually gives the right balance of coverage and resolution.
Does this work on mobile phones?
Yes. ToolsNest is fully mobile-responsive and works on Android and iOS browsers. You can take a photo directly with your phone camera and immediately upload it to extract text — without installing any app.
