How to Use

  1. Click "Select Image" to upload a JPG, PNG, or WebP image from your device.
  2. Choose your desired grid layout: 3x1 (3 pieces), 3x2 (6 pieces), or 3x3 (9 pieces).
  3. Preview the grid to see how your image will be split, with numbered pieces showing the correct Instagram upload order.
  4. Click "Download All Pieces" to save each piece as a numbered PNG file.
  5. Upload the pieces to Instagram in order, starting from piece 1, to complete your grid.

Frequently Asked Questions

How does the Instagram grid work?

Instagram displays your profile photos in a 3-column grid. By splitting a large image into 3, 6, or 9 equal pieces and uploading them in the correct order, you create a seamless panoramic or mosaic effect on your profile page.

What order should I upload the pieces?

Upload the pieces in reverse order, starting from the highest number down to 1. The last piece you upload (piece 1) will appear in the top-left of your Instagram grid. Each piece is numbered to guide you.

Are my images uploaded to a server?

No. All image processing is done entirely in your browser using the HTML5 Canvas API. Your images stay on your device and are never sent to any server.

What image format are the pieces saved as?

All grid pieces are saved as high-quality PNG files to preserve image clarity and detail, which is important for Instagram's compression.

What image size works best?

For best results, use a high-resolution image. A 3x3 grid on Instagram displays at roughly 1080x1080 pixels total, so an image of at least 1080 pixels wide is recommended. Larger images will produce sharper results.
