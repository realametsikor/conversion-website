This tool supports five QR code types. Each encodes different data and triggers a different action when scanned.

🔗

URL

Opens a website or web page when scanned. The most common QR code type.

📝

Text

📐 QR Code Size Guide — Which Size Should You Choose?

The right size depends on where and how your QR code will be used. Choosing the wrong size can make it unscannable in print or oversized on screen.

Size Best For Min. Scan Distance Recommended
200 × 200 px Website embeds, email signatures, digital documents ~10 cm Digital use
300 × 300 px Business cards, flyers, product labels ~15 cm Most versatile
400 × 400 px A4 posters, brochures, menus, event materials ~25 cm Print standard
500 × 500 px Large format print, banners, signage, displays ~35 cm Large print only
Print tip: For physical print materials, always use at least 300 × 300px and ensure the printed QR code is at least 2cm × 2cm in physical size. Anything smaller becomes difficult to scan reliably.

✅ Tips for Creating Effective QR Codes

  • 1
    Always test before printing. Scan your QR code with at least two different phones before committing to a print run. What works on one scanner may not work on another if contrast is too low.
  • 2
    Keep URLs short. Longer URLs create denser, more complex QR codes that are harder to scan. Use a URL shortener if your link is very long.
  • 3
    Maintain high contrast. Dark on light always works best. Avoid light-coloured foregrounds on white backgrounds — scanners struggle with low contrast codes.
  • 4
    Add a call to action near the QR code. "Scan to visit our menu" or "Scan to connect to Wi-Fi" tells people what to expect. QR codes without context get ignored.
  • 5
    Leave white space around the QR code. QR codes need a clear border (called a quiet zone) around them to scan correctly. Don't crop the code too tightly in your design.
  • 6
    Make sure the URL still works. Static QR codes permanently encode whatever URL you enter. If the webpage moves or goes offline, the code will scan but lead to a dead link.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

Can I scan the generated QR codes with any phone?
Yes. QR codes generated by this tool follow the international QR code standard (ISO/IEC 18004) and can be scanned by any modern smartphone camera or QR code scanning app on both iOS and Android. Most phone cameras can scan QR codes natively without a separate app.
Do QR codes expire?
Static QR codes generated by this tool never expire. The QR code permanently encodes whatever content you enter — URL, text, Wi-Fi credentials, or phone number. However, if the URL your QR code points to goes offline or changes, the code will scan successfully but lead to a dead or wrong page. Always make sure the destination URL is permanent before printing.
How much data can a QR code hold?
QR codes can store up to approximately 2,953 bytes of binary data, 4,296 alphanumeric characters, or 7,089 numeric digits. For practical use, short URLs (under 100 characters) and brief text work best for reliable scanning. Very long URLs create dense QR codes that are harder to scan, especially when printed small.
Can I customise the colours of my QR code?
Yes. You can change both the foreground (dark module) colour and the background colour using the colour pickers. Always ensure strong contrast between the two colours — dark foreground on a light background is the most reliable combination. Avoid inverting (light on dark) as many scanners struggle with this.
Is my data private when generating QR codes?
Absolutely. QR codes are generated entirely in your browser using JavaScript. No data is sent to any server. Your URLs, Wi-Fi passwords, email addresses, and phone numbers are never stored or transmitted. Everything stays on your device.
What size QR code should I use for print?
For business cards and flyers, use 300 × 300px and ensure the printed size is at least 2cm × 2cm. For A4 posters, use 400 × 400px. For large banners and signage, use 500 × 500px. The larger the physical print size, the easier it is to scan from a distance.
Can I use the QR code for commercial purposes?
Yes. QR codes generated by ToolsNest are yours to use however you like — on business cards, product packaging, marketing materials, websites, and any commercial application. There are no usage restrictions or licensing fees.
Does this work on mobile phones?
Yes. ToolsNest is fully mobile-responsive. You can generate and download QR codes directly from your phone browser without installing any app — useful when you need a Wi-Fi QR code quickly on the go.
