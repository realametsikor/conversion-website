How to Use

  1. Enter your target savings goal amount in dollars (e.g., $50,000 for a down payment).
  2. Enter how much you currently have saved toward this goal.
  3. Enter the amount you plan to contribute each month.
  4. Enter the expected annual interest rate on your savings, then click "Calculate Time to Goal" to see when you will reach your target.

Frequently Asked Questions

How does compound interest affect my savings timeline?

Compound interest means you earn interest on both your principal and previously earned interest. Over time, this creates a snowball effect that accelerates your savings growth. Even a modest interest rate can significantly shorten the time to reach your goal, especially over longer periods.

What interest rate should I use?

Use the annual percentage yield (APY) offered by your savings account or investment. High-yield savings accounts typically offer 3-5% APY, certificates of deposit (CDs) may offer 4-5%, and diversified investment portfolios have historically averaged 7-10% annually, though with more risk.

Does this calculator account for taxes on interest?

No. Interest earned in taxable accounts is subject to income tax. The actual time to reach your goal may be slightly longer if taxes reduce your effective interest rate. Consider using tax-advantaged accounts like IRAs or 401(k)s when possible.

What if my current savings already exceed the goal?

If your current savings are already equal to or greater than your goal, the calculator will let you know that you have already reached your savings target. Congratulations!
