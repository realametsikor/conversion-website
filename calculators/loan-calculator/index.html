How to Use

  1. Enter the total loan amount you plan to borrow in dollars.
  2. Enter the annual interest rate as a percentage (e.g., 6.5 for 6.5%).
  3. Enter the loan term in years (e.g., 30 for a 30-year mortgage).
  4. Click "Calculate" to see your estimated monthly payment, total interest, and total payment.

Frequently Asked Questions

What formula does this calculator use?

This calculator uses the standard amortization formula: M = P[r(1+r)^n] / [(1+r)^n - 1], where M is the monthly payment, P is the principal, r is the monthly interest rate, and n is the total number of payments.

Does this include taxes and insurance?

No. This calculator computes principal and interest only. Your actual monthly payment may be higher when property taxes, homeowner's insurance, and PMI are included.

Can I use this for different types of loans?

Yes. This calculator works for mortgages, auto loans, personal loans, student loans, and any other fixed-rate amortizing loan.

What if my interest rate is 0%?

If the interest rate is 0%, the calculator will simply divide the loan amount by the total number of months to determine the monthly payment, with zero total interest.

Tips for Managing Your Loan Effectively

  • 1
    Shop for the lowest interest rate before committing. Even 0.5% difference in rate can save thousands over the life of a loan. Get quotes from at least 3 lenders and compare the APR, not just the interest rate.
  • 2
    Make extra payments when possible. Any amount paid above your minimum monthly payment goes directly to reducing your principal — dramatically cutting total interest.
  • 3
    Understand APR vs interest rate. The Annual Percentage Rate (APR) includes fees and other costs, making it the true cost of borrowing. Always compare APR when evaluating loan offers.
  • 4
    Improve your credit score before applying. Borrowers with higher credit scores qualify for significantly lower interest rates. Even a 50-point improvement can save thousands over the life of a large loan.
  • 5
    Use this calculator to stress-test your budget. Calculate what your monthly payment would be if rates rise by 1–2%. If the higher payment would strain your budget, consider a smaller loan or longer term as a buffer.

Frequently Asked Questions

What formula does this loan calculator use?
This calculator uses the standard amortisation formula: M = P × [r(1+r)^n] ÷ [(1+r)^n - 1], where M is the monthly payment, P is the principal loan amount, r is the monthly interest rate (annual rate divided by 12), and n is the total number of monthly payments (years × 12). This is the same formula used by banks worldwide.
Does this include taxes and insurance?
No. This calculator computes principal and interest only. For mortgage loans, your actual monthly payment will be higher when property taxes, homeowner's insurance, and private mortgage insurance (PMI) are included. Contact your lender for a full breakdown.
Can I use this for different types of loans?
Yes. This calculator works for any fixed-rate amortising loan — including mortgages, auto loans, personal loans, student loans, and business loans. It assumes equal monthly payments and a fixed interest rate for the entire loan term.
What is amortisation?
Amortisation is the process of paying off a loan through regular equal monthly payments. Each payment covers both the interest owed for that month and a portion of the principal balance. Early payments are mostly interest; later payments are mostly principal.
Should I choose a 15-year or 30-year mortgage?
A 15-year mortgage has higher monthly payments but saves significantly on total interest — often $100,000 or more on a standard loan. A 30-year mortgage has lower monthly payments, giving you more monthly cash flow flexibility. Use this calculator to compare both options with your specific loan amount and rate.
Is my financial data private?
Yes — completely. All calculations happen entirely in your browser. Your loan amounts and financial figures are never sent to any server or stored anywhere.
What if my interest rate is 0%?
If the interest rate is 0%, the calculator divides the loan amount by the total number of months to calculate the monthly payment, with zero total interest. This applies to some promotional financing offers on vehicles and electronics.
