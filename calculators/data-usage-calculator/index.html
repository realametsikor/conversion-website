Frequently Asked Questions

How accurate are these data usage estimates?

These estimates are based on average data consumption rates published by major streaming services and internet providers. Actual usage can vary depending on the specific platform, content quality settings, compression, and other factors. Use these figures as a planning guideline.

Why does 4K streaming use so much more data than HD?

4K (Ultra HD) video has four times the pixel count of 1080p HD, which means significantly more data is needed to transmit each frame. A typical 4K stream uses about 7 GB per hour compared to roughly 3 GB per hour for HD and 0.7 GB per hour for SD.

Does online gaming use a lot of data?

Online gaming typically uses less data than most people expect, averaging around 40-150 MB per hour for most games. However, game downloads and updates can be very large (often 20-100+ GB). This calculator estimates ongoing gameplay data usage only, not downloads.

How can I reduce my data usage?

Lower video streaming quality, disable auto-play, use Wi-Fi whenever possible, limit background app refresh, download content for offline use, and compress images before uploading. Most streaming apps allow you to set a preferred quality to help manage data usage.
