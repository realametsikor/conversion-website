How to Convert MP4 to MP3 for Free

Published on March 2, 2026 · 5 min read

Converting MP4 to MP3 extracts the audio track from a video file. This is useful when you want to listen to music, podcasts, lectures, or interviews without the video. A free online converter handles the process in seconds with no software to install.

What Is MP4 to MP3 Conversion?

MP4 is a video file format that contains both video and audio data. MP3 is an audio-only format. When you convert MP4 to MP3, the tool strips out the video portion and saves just the audio as a standalone file.

The resulting MP3 file is much smaller than the original MP4 because it only contains the audio track. A 100 MB video might produce a 5-10 MB MP3 file.

Why You Might Need It

  • Listen to lectures on the go: Extract audio from recorded lectures or presentations to listen during commutes or workouts.
  • Save storage space: Audio files are much smaller than video files. Convert videos you only need to hear, not watch.
  • Create podcasts or clips: Pull audio from interviews, webinars, or conference talks to repurpose as podcast content.
  • Make ringtones: Extract a specific audio segment from a music video to use as a phone ringtone.
  • Play on audio devices: Some portable players, car stereos, and smart speakers only support audio formats like MP3.

Step-by-Step Guide

Tips and Best Practices

  • Choose the right bitrate. For spoken content like lectures and podcasts, 128 kbps is fine. For music, use 192 kbps or higher for better sound quality.
  • Check the source quality. The MP3 output cannot sound better than the original audio in the MP4 file. A low-quality video will produce a low-quality audio file.
  • Trim before converting. If you only need a portion of the audio, trim the video first to avoid converting unnecessary content.
  • Use for personal content. Only convert videos that you own or have permission to use. Respect copyright when extracting audio from third-party content.
  • Test on your playback device. After downloading, play the MP3 on the device you plan to use. Verify that the audio sounds clear and complete.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does converting MP4 to MP3 lose quality?

There is minimal quality loss during conversion. The audio track is re-encoded as MP3, which is a lossy format. At 192 kbps or higher, most people cannot hear the difference from the original.

What is the best bitrate for MP3?

128 kbps works well for speech and casual listening. 192 kbps is a good balance of quality and file size. 320 kbps is the highest standard MP3 bitrate and is best for music where quality matters.

Can I convert large video files?

Most free online converters handle files up to 50-100 MB. For larger files, try compressing your file first or use a desktop application.

Is the conversion private?

ToolsNest processes files in the browser when possible and does not store your files after conversion. Your content stays private.

What other audio formats can I convert to?

MP3 is the most widely supported audio format. It works on virtually every device and music player. For most use cases, MP3 is the best choice for converted audio.
