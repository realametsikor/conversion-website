How to Convert Excel to PDF Online for Free

Published on March 2, 2026 · 5 min read

Sharing an Excel spreadsheet as a PDF ensures that your formatting, formulas, and layout look the same on every device. This guide walks you through converting Excel files to PDF for free, with tips for keeping your spreadsheets looking professional.

Why Convert Excel to PDF?

There are several good reasons to save your spreadsheets as PDFs:

  • Preserve formatting: PDFs display identically on every device. Excel files may shift columns or change fonts depending on the software used to open them.
  • Protect data: PDFs prevent recipients from accidentally editing your formulas or data. The spreadsheet becomes read-only.
  • Professional sharing: Financial reports, invoices, and data summaries look polished as PDFs when shared with clients or managers.
  • Reduce file size: PDF versions of spreadsheets are often smaller than the original Excel files, especially for large workbooks.
  • No software required: Recipients do not need Microsoft Excel or Google Sheets to view a PDF.

Tips for Better Excel to PDF Conversion

  • Set print area first: Before converting, select the cells you want included and set the print area in Excel. This prevents blank pages or cut-off columns in the PDF.
  • Use landscape for wide spreadsheets: If your spreadsheet has many columns, landscape orientation prevents columns from being split across pages.
  • Adjust column widths: Make sure all data is visible within cells. Hidden overflow text will be cut off in the PDF.
  • Freeze header rows: While frozen panes do not transfer to PDF, you can use the "Print Titles" feature in Excel to repeat header rows on every page.
  • Remove gridlines if not needed: Gridlines can make PDFs look cluttered. Remove them for a cleaner appearance if the data does not require a grid.
  • Check for multiple sheets: If your workbook has multiple tabs, verify which sheets are included in the PDF conversion.
