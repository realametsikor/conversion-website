Tips for Writing a Great Cover Letter

  • Personalise the opening. If you know the hiring manager's name, use it. "Dear Ms. Adu" is always stronger than "Dear Hiring Manager."
  • Lead with your value, not your intent. Don't open with "I am applying for..." — open with your strongest relevant skill or achievement.
  • Mirror the job description. Use keywords from the job posting. Applicant tracking systems (ATS) scan for these exact phrases.
  • Keep it to one page. 250–400 words is ideal. Hiring managers don't read long cover letters — they skim them.
  • End with a clear call to action. Request an interview or state your availability. "I'd welcome the opportunity to discuss this further" is stronger than trailing off.
  • Always personalise the AI output. Add one specific detail about the company — a recent project, their mission, or something you genuinely admire. It shows real interest.
  • Proofread before sending. Run your cover letter through our Grammar Checker to catch any errors before submitting.

Common Cover Letter Mistakes to Avoid

  • Repeating your entire resume. Your cover letter should complement your resume, not duplicate it. Focus on context and motivation, not a list of jobs.
  • Being too generic. A cover letter that could apply to any company impresses no one. Reference the specific role and company.
  • Focusing on what the job does for you. Employers want to know what you bring — not that this role fits your career goals.
  • Using clichés. Phrases like "I am a team player" and "I am passionate about this opportunity" are overused and meaningless without evidence.
  • Wrong formatting. Walls of text are skipped. Use short paragraphs, clear structure, and white space.
