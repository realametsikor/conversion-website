How to Use

  1. Enter your original headline in the text field above. It can be a blog title, email subject line, social media post, or any heading.
  2. Select your target audience from the dropdown to tailor the tone and vocabulary of the rewrites.
  3. Click "Rewrite Headline" to instantly generate 8 different variations in styles such as SEO-optimized, question-based, listicle, emotional, and more.
  4. Review the analysis of your original headline including word count, emotional score, readability grade, and detected power words.
  5. Click the copy button on any variation to copy it to your clipboard for immediate use.

Frequently Asked Questions

How does the headline rewriter generate variations?

The tool uses a client-side NLP-style algorithm that parses your headline to identify the subject, verb, and object. It then applies eight distinct rewriting strategies -- SEO optimization, question formatting, how-to conversion, listicle numbering, emotional power words, curiosity gaps, benefit-driven framing, and direct conciseness -- to produce unique variations tailored to your chosen audience.

What is the emotional score and how is it calculated?

The emotional score ranges from 0 to 100 and measures how emotionally compelling your headline is. It is calculated based on the density of power words (words scientifically shown to trigger emotional responses) relative to total word count. Headlines scoring above 50 tend to generate significantly higher click-through rates.

Is my data private when using this tool?

Yes, completely. This headline rewriter runs entirely in your browser using client-side JavaScript. No text is sent to any server, no API calls are made, and nothing is stored. Your headlines remain fully private.

What types of headlines work best with this tool?

The tool works well with blog post titles, email subject lines, social media captions, YouTube video titles, ad copy headlines, and landing page headers. For best results, enter headlines that are at least 4-5 words long and convey a clear topic or message.
