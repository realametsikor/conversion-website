Frequently Asked Questions

What is the STAR method for resume bullets?

The STAR method structures accomplishments as: Situation/Task, Action, and Result. For resume bullets, this translates to starting with a strong action verb, describing what you did and how you did it, and ending with a measurable outcome. This format helps recruiters quickly understand your impact.

Why are action verbs important in resume bullets?

Strong action verbs like "Spearheaded," "Orchestrated," and "Optimized" immediately convey leadership and initiative. They replace weak phrases like "Responsible for" or "Helped with," making your accomplishments sound more impactful and professional to hiring managers and ATS systems.

Does this tool send my data to any server?

No. This tool runs entirely in your browser using client-side JavaScript. Your job details, descriptions, and generated bullets never leave your device. No API calls, no data collection, completely private.

How long should a resume bullet point be?

Ideal resume bullets are 1-2 lines long (roughly 80-160 characters). This tool includes a character counter for each bullet to help you stay within ATS-friendly limits. Bullets that are too long tend to lose the reader's attention.

Can I use these bullets for any industry?

Yes. The generator includes industry-specific templates and vocabulary for Technology, Healthcare, Finance, Marketing, Education, Engineering, Sales, and Operations. Select your industry for the most relevant phrasing, or choose "General" for versatile bullets.
