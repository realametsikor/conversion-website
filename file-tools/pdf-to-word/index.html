What Is a PDF to Word Converter?

A PDF to Word converter transforms a static, read-only PDF document into a fully editable Microsoft Word file (.docx). Once converted, you can change text, update figures, reformat paragraphs, add comments, and save in any Word-compatible app including Google Docs, LibreOffice, and Apple Pages.

Who uses this most? Students editing downloaded lecture notes, professionals updating contracts they only have as PDFs, job seekers converting their CV back to editable format, and anyone who received a PDF form they need to fill in as a Word document.

Text-Based PDF vs Scanned PDF — Know the Difference

Not all PDFs are the same. Understanding which type you have sets your expectations for conversion quality.

Excellent conversion

Text-Based PDF

Created digitally. Text is actual data — you can click and highlight it in your PDF viewer.

  • Fonts and headings convert cleanly
  • Tables and columns preserved
  • Images stay in place
  • Near-perfect output quality
Needs OCR first

Scanned PDF

A photograph of a document. Text appears as an image — you cannot select it.

  • Use Image to Text (OCR) tool first
  • Extract text then paste into Word
  • Accuracy depends on image quality
  • Formatting needs manual rebuilding
Quick test: Open your PDF and try clicking and dragging to select some text. If you can highlight it, it's text-based and will convert beautifully. If you can't select anything, it's scanned — use our Image to Text tool first.

Tips for Best Conversion Results

  • 1
    Test if your PDF is text-based first. Try selecting text in your PDF viewer before converting. If you can highlight it, the conversion will produce excellent results.
  • 2
    Keep file size under 10MB for fastest results. Large files with many images take longer. Compress large PDFs first using our PDF Compressor.
  • 3
    Check tables after conversion. Complex multi-column or merged-cell tables may need minor manual adjustments in Word.
  • 4
    Use a modern browser. Chrome or Edge give the most reliable conversion results.
  • 5
    For scanned PDFs, use OCR first. Run your scanned page through our Image to Text tool to extract the text, then paste into a new Word document.
  • 6
    Never upload confidential files to unknown converters. ToolsNest processes everything locally — your files never leave your device.
💡 Pro tip: After converting, use Word's "Compare Documents" feature (Review → Compare) to quickly spot any formatting differences between your original PDF layout and the converted Word file.

Are my files secure?
Yes. Files are processed entirely in your browser and never uploaded to any server. Your documents stay completely private — even confidential files like contracts and medical records are safe.
Will the formatting be preserved?
For text-based PDFs, formatting is preserved closely — including fonts, headings, paragraph spacing, images, and most tables. Complex layouts like multi-column PDFs may need minor manual adjustments in Word after conversion.
What is the maximum file size?
You can convert PDF files up to 50MB. If your file exceeds this, use our PDF Compressor to reduce the file size before converting.
Can I convert scanned PDFs?
Scanned PDFs are images of text, not actual text data. Use our Image to Text (OCR) tool to extract the text first, then paste it into a new Word document.
Can I convert the Word file back to PDF after editing?
Yes. After editing in Word or Google Docs, use our Word to PDF converter to turn it back into a clean, professional PDF.
What is the difference between .doc and .docx?
.docx is the modern Microsoft Word format supported by all modern word processors including Google Docs, LibreOffice, and Apple Pages. ToolsNest outputs .docx, which works universally across all platforms.
Does this work on mobile phones?
Yes. ToolsNest is fully mobile-responsive and works on Android and iOS browsers. Convert PDF files directly from your phone without installing any app.
