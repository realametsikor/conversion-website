How to Use

  1. Set your preferred focus duration, break lengths, and number of sessions in Settings.
  2. Optionally type what you're working on in the task field.
  3. Click "Start" to begin your focus session. The timer counts down with a visual ring.
  4. When the timer ends, a notification sound plays and your break begins (if auto-start is on).
  5. After completing all sessions, a long break starts. Your stats track sessions, focus time, and streaks.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the Pomodoro Technique?

Developed by Francesco Cirillo in the late 1980s, the Pomodoro Technique uses a timer to break work into intervals (traditionally 25 minutes) separated by short breaks. After four intervals, you take a longer break. This helps maintain focus and prevent burnout.

Can I customize the timer durations?

Yes! Adjust focus time (1-120 minutes), short break (1-30 minutes), long break (1-60 minutes), and the number of sessions before a long break (1-10). Settings are saved in your browser.

Are my statistics saved?

Your daily statistics and session logs are stored in your browser's local storage. They persist between sessions on the same device but are never uploaded to any server.

Does the timer work if I switch tabs?

Yes, the timer continues running in the background. You'll receive a sound notification when each interval ends (if sound is enabled). The page title also updates with the remaining time.
