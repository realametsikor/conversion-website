How to Use

  1. Create: Add cards one at a time or bulk-import by pasting "front | back" pairs.
  2. Study: Flip through cards, rate your knowledge (Again/Hard/Good/Easy), and the tool prioritizes cards you struggle with.
  3. Manage: View all cards in a deck, edit or delete individual cards, and export decks.
  4. Organize cards into multiple decks for different subjects.
  5. All data is saved locally in your browser and persists between sessions.

Frequently Asked Questions

How does spaced repetition work?

When you rate a card, the tool adjusts how soon you'll see it again. Cards you mark as "Again" reappear quickly, while "Easy" cards appear less frequently. This scientifically proven method optimizes long-term retention.

Where are my flashcards stored?

All flashcards are saved in your browser's local storage. They persist between sessions on the same device but are never uploaded to any server. Use the Export feature to back up your decks.

Can I import existing flashcards?

Yes! Use the Bulk Import feature on the Create tab. Paste your cards in "front | back" format, one card per line. You can also use Tab as a separator instead of the pipe (|) character.

Is there a limit to how many cards I can create?

There's no artificial limit. The practical limit depends on your browser's local storage capacity, which is typically around 5-10MB — enough for thousands of text-based flashcards.
