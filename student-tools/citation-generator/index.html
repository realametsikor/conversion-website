How to Use

  1. Select your citation style (APA, MLA, Chicago, or Harvard) and source type (book, journal, website, etc.).
  2. Fill in the source details. Required fields are marked with an asterisk (*).
  3. Click "Generate Citation" to create your formatted citation.
  4. Use "Show All Formats" to see the citation in all four styles at once.
  5. Citations are automatically saved in your browser. Use "Export All" to download your bibliography.

Frequently Asked Questions

Which citation style should I use?

APA is standard for psychology, education, and social sciences. MLA is used in humanities and liberal arts. Chicago is common in history, business, and fine arts. Harvard is widely used in the UK and Australia across many disciplines. Check your assignment guidelines or ask your instructor.

How accurate are the citations?

The generator follows official style guide rules for each format. However, always double-check citations against your institution's specific requirements, as some professors or departments may have variations.

Are my citations saved?

Citations are saved in your browser's local storage so they persist between sessions on the same device. They are never uploaded to any server. Use the Export button to download all citations as a text file.

Can I generate in-text citations?

Currently, this tool generates full reference list / bibliography entries. In-text citation formats are shown alongside each reference for quick guidance.
